# Learnings

- A deck plan becomes useful only after the reader and next decision are named.
- Prerequisites must not masquerade as pages.
- Structured choices beat blank boxes when the product cannot interpret prose.
- “Film and TV only” is a strength: narrow evidence creates better decisions.
- Quick routes need visible assumptions; expert routes should replace them, not merely add ceremony.
- A hidden conditional answer is still user data; when its parent fact changes, the answer must leave too.
- Horizontal deck navigation needs exact pages and visible position—not approximate scrolling.
- A useful export must preserve “do this first,” not quietly flatten every answer into pages.
